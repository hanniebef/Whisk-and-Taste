GRANDMA'S
PUMPKIN PIE

A golden, flaky-crusted pumpkin pie, rich and creamy with a velvety filling of roasted pumpkin, cinnamon, nutmeg, and a hint of brown sugar. Each bite melts in your mouth, with warm, comforting spices balanced to perfection.

PREP TIME

15 mins

COOK TIME

45 mins

SERVINGS

8 Slices

Ingredients
Crust:
  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter (cold, cut into cubes)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 3-4 tablespoons ice water
Filling:
  • 2 cups pumpkin puree (canned or fresh)
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Pinch of salt
Instructions
For the Crust:

In a bowl, mix flour and salt. Cut in the cold butter using a pastry cutter or fork until it resembles coarse crumbs. Add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together. Shape the dough into a disk, wrap it in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. Roll out the chilled dough on a lightly floured surface and place it in a 9-inch pie dish. Trim and crimp the edges as desired.


For the Filling:

Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, whisk together the pumpkin puree, brown sugar, eggs, heavy cream, milk, and vanilla until smooth. Add the cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves, ginger, and salt. Mix until fully combined.


Assemble and Bake:

Pour the filling into the prepared pie crust. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the centre is just set (a slight jiggle is okay). If the crust edges start browning too quickly, cover them with foil. Remove from the oven and let the pie cool on a wire rack for at least 2 hours to set properly.
